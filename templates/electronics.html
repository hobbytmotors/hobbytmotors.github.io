Control electronics and software

The control electronics can be split into two main components, of-the-shelf and project specific. Control over the electric motor/generators is to be delegated to a specialized company and their products. The remainder of the control electronics is to be built from scratch using an Arduino microprocessor and sensory. Project specific control is required to monitor and operate throttle and brake inputs, variator ratio, speed and combination of electric and petrol power. The control system is also going to feature a project specific traction control system. Closely monitoring the driver inputs in the form of throttle position, steering angle and vehicle speed we can vary the transmission ratio of each wheel in order to make the car steer better. Slowing the rotational velocity of the inside wheels and increasing that of the outside ones, helps the vehicle perform the arc of the corner desired by the driver. The control over the ratio of the variator transmissions is applied through a couple of linear actuators on each axis. Furthermore, the distribution of power to and from the onboard sources will be handled by the bespoke control algorithms in the microprocessor. Since the driver only has one throttle pedal, but the motion is governed by two electric and one petrol engine, the distribution of work between them depends on the control system under various driving conditions. Another task of the microprocessor is to distribute the source of the electric power between the batteries and the flywheels also driven by electric motor/generator. Under certain conditions the flywheel can be a power sink, while the batteries are the source and vice versa.

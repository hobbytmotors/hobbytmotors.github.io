Suspension

Regardless of the technical innovation and low cost targets in the project a vehicle needs to be comfortable, easy and safe to operate for the passengers. This is an issue with low weight constructions. The lighter the body, the greater the effect of road surface and cornering forces on it, leading to bumpy and choppy ride. Installing soft springs and shock absorbers solves the comfort problem, but make the vehicle difficult to safely hold the road and unpleasant to drive. The key to solving both comfort and handling is the light components after the spring and shock absorber, i.e. the suspension, brakes and wheels and their correct geometry. Since the front and rear of the chassis is narrow as a result of the aerodynamic shape and the wheels placed at a standard distance for stability, conventional strut tower Macpherson setup was not applicable. The layout of the chassis and wheels bares a close resemblance to single-seater formula race cars. Thus we adopted their suspension setup – unequal length double wishbone suspension and pushrod coilovers positioned inside the main chassis body. An exemplary drawing presented below:

The setup has a number of benefits in road holding ability at various angles of lean and vertical wheel displacement. Simply put, it keeps as mush of the tire surface in contact with the road at as many various conditions as possible. Last but not least of the components that move with the wheel in conventional vehicles and thus increasing the unsprung (away from the spring and absorber from the chassis) weight are the brake discs and calipers. Having all wheel drive means we have a driveshaft going from the chassis to each wheel. This makes possible the use of an initially unnatural solution – inboard brakes. This system moves the caliper and disc from the wheel end of the driveshaft to the chassis end of it. In this way, the brake force acts on the driveshaft which in turn acts on the wheel and tire. This brings a number of benefits, from lower unsprung weight, through better protection from the elements for the brakes and even better weight distribution. Less weight on the end of the car body means its easier to rotate around its axes, which leads to better cornering and more stable road holding.

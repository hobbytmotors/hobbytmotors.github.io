Systems

Our team has divided its efforts in several critical subsystems in order to achieve the targeted results

Force Balance

Albert Einstein famously defined insanity as “doing the same thing over and over again and expecting different results”. We feel this is what is happening with the automotive industry lately. With our limited experience in automotive design we started from zero, which in a physics problem is the force balance on a body in motion. We looked at what makes a car go, stop and turn in order to achieve those more efficiently.

Drivetrain

The drivetrain is the series of mechanical components that converts whatever form of energy used for motion into the movement of the vehicle. In this project it has two main tasks, dynamics of motion that is more than adequate for modern standards and the efficient use of energy that is associated with motion.

Batteries and BMS

After a number of simulations and considerations electricity was chosen as an auxiliary form of driving and stopping energy to improve the energy efficiency of motion. The storage of this form of energy is batteries, which form an important piece of the performance, maintenance, durability, safety characteristics and cost of the project. To their aid we have designed a relatively low rpm flywheel to supplement the positive and negative operating characteristics of batteries.

Electronics & Software

The combination of petrol and electric power units in a unique body shape and chassis demand that they are controlled as precise as possible. As much as we aim to use standard parts and of-the-shelf component, when they are brought together they require specific instructions to operate as we like them to. Control electronics in the form of sensors, signal processing and electromechanical actuators, achieve this harmony of components.

Chassis

The biggest are of gains to be made are the aerodynamics and weight of a vehicle in order to make the drivetrain and other systems as efficient and cost-effective as possible. The shape we developed with hired CFD software achieves excellent drag characteristics. The respective low weight is achieved by making small compromises with space and seating inside the vehicle and using cost-effective materials of high properties.

Suspensions

Regardless of all other improvements, the aim of a car is to go along the road. Its contact with the road is through the wheels, tires and suspension. The low weight and specific shape put requirements on the suspension which are fairly specific to the vehicle. Passenger comfort, excellent road holding and safety are crucial to the successful acceptance of the prototype with the public and potential investors. Just like with the rest of the vehicle, the solutions are unorthodox but effective.

Story of the Project

Some sub text

October 2012

Conceptual Design

A concept of making a cheap personal vehicle was conceived. As young professionals each in a different line of work we wanted to travel in-town cheaper than what cars at the time offered. Cheap old car and high petrol consumption or expensive newer cars with lesser consumption were the only options. What about new, cheap and low consumption, how hard could it be?!

January 2013 to August 2014

First Attempts

What started as a hobby became a full scale conversion project of a Fiat Seicento to first an electric and then petrol-electric hybrid. Cost was to be minimal as everything was personally funded. We aimed to source components locally while still constructing the car with decent performance and safety.

April 2014

Lessons Learned

During the conversion of the first prototype we learned a lot about how difficult setting up and operating the components were. With a fairly significant fire due to an overheated motor controller and a resulting spectacular short circuit arc, the project car was damaged, but our spirit unhindered. We had to start anew, but with a lot of lessons learned.

June 2014 to August 2014

Daring to look further

The mathematical model developed in the conceptual stage proved to be accurate with the testing of the converted Seicento. We immediately started thinking “what if we make it …, what if we use this component” and so on. The result is the prototype 2 vehicle. A radically different shape and characteristics by using the principles applied in the first prototype emerged.

September 2014

Step by Step

With a developed brand, colors, logo and website we started to dare make this hobby into a more professional endeavor. While still personally funded we started drawings and component sourcing for the second prototype. The future of the project depends on attracting investment with the successful completion of prototype II. Cheap, energy efficient vehicle – how hard can it be …
